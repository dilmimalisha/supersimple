Environment 

The environment encompasses the natural surroundings in which living organisms, including humans, exist. It consists of the air, water, land, and the complex web of interactions between them. The environment provides essential resources, such as clean air, fresh water, fertile soil, and a diverse range of ecosystems that support life.

Why should we protect the environment? 
There are several compelling reasons why we should protect the environment: Ecological Balance: The environment is a complex web of interdependent ecosystems and species. By protecting it, we maintain the delicate balance that allows life to thrive on Earth. Protecting biodiversity ensures the stability and resilience of ecosystems, which in turn benefits human well-being. Human Health: A clean and healthy environment is essential for our well-being. Environmental degradation can lead to air and water pollution, soil contamination, and the spread of diseases. By safeguarding the environment, we protect ourselves from these harmful impacts and promote a healthier and safer living environment. Natural Resources: The environment provides us with valuable resources such as clean air, water, food, and energy. By protecting these resources, we ensure their sustainable availability for present and future generations. Conserving natural resources also helps maintain economic stability and supports industries like agriculture, forestry, and tourism.
